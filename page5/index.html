Environmental Pollution

Although not many are aware, environmental pollution is very common. Some common examples are throwing gums on the ground or littering. To few, it may seem like a small litter and does not affect much however wildlife may take them as food which may be hazards for them. Most commonly heard of environmental pollution will be at beaches as tourists or people going for picnic leave stuff without cleaning up after their activities. Some of these waste are left behind and kill aquatic wildlife and can increase the risk of gems infecting livestocks that we humans eat.

In summary, reducing environmental pollution can benefit not us human well-beings, but the planet as well. It can provide better air quality, preventing respiratory diseases or cardiovascular conditions and can also prevent bacterias from mutating and evolving which can cause planetary pandemics.
